Dear Independent Submissions Editor,

I am submitting "CWIP: Human-Readable, Machine-Decodable IPv6 over
Continuous Wave (and Flashing Lights)" for consideration as an
April 1 RFC.

The premise: carry IPv6 packets over Morse code (CW keying), the
oldest digital mode, while keeping transmissions human-recognizable
at the edges and machine-efficient in the middle. Operators can
supervise bursts via traditional Morse prosigns, while the payload
uses self-clocking line codes, Reed-Solomon FEC, and interleaving
for robust machine decoding.

The joke is taking the most venerable radio technology (CW, dating
to the 1840s) and the most modern network protocol (IPv6) and
making them work together with full technical rigor. Every detail
is implementable - the framing, the FEC parameters, the line coding
- it just happens to achieve roughly 1 kbps on a good day.

The draft is available as a GitHub gist in mmark markdown format:
https://gist.github.com/MarkAtwood/a3ac2b631dfc267dd3d271bd0f0c2343

I can provide xml2rfc XML output on request.

A companion draft, "Ham64: IPv6-Embedded Human Identifier Encoding,"
defines an addressing scheme for embedding callsigns, tail numbers,
and other human identifiers directly in IPv6 addresses. I am
submitting it separately but they work well together.

Thank you for your consideration.
